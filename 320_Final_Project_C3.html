Introduction:



Spotify is a international major music streaming platform and it generates massive amounts of data from the millions of artists, hundreds of millions of songs, and numerous listening preferences. The popularity of a song/music track is a very important metric and is influenced by how widely and how frequently the song or track is streamed by the users.

This project explores and addresses the major factors that may influence the popularity of a music track. The spotify dataset used in this project holds meaningiful and useful information about the songs like the length of duration of the song, the genre of the song, the artist who created the song, the album from which the song is from or a part of, and if the song is of explicit nature.

This project is centered around the follwowing major questions:

What does the distribution of song popularity on Spotify look like?, Does a relation exists between the length of the song/track and its respective popularity?, Are music tracks of the explicit nature more popular than the average music tracks?, Can ML models classify whether a song will become popular using track features?

The aforementioned questions are important and relevant since understanding user listening behavior and music trends is crucial for people and entities such as music producers, artists, music label organizations, and platforms to continue creating popular music, promoting their work, and classifying if the track or song they created will become popular. The intention of this project is to use machine learning models and techniques along with data analysis tools to evaluate how effectively songs can be classified as popular or not using track features.

So I performed a one sample t test with signifcance level alpha = 0.5 and I visualzied using a histrogram.

My Null Hypothesis was mu = 30.75, which means that the mean popularity of explcit tracks is the same as the overall mean

My alternative hypothesis was mu > 30.75, which means that the mean popularity of explicit tracks is greater than the overall mean.


Observe that the mean popularity of explicit tracks is 33.67 while the mean popularity of the overall tracks is 30.75 and 33.67 > 30.75

After doing the test, see that the p value is extremely small, much much less than 0.05, so I rejecet the null hypothesis.

This means there is strong statsitical evidence that explicit tracks have higher popularity then the tracks in the overall dataset.
